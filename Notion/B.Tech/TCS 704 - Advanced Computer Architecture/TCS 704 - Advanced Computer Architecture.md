# Moore’s Law
Moore's Law states that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles about every two years, though the cost of computers is halved.
- In 1965, Gordon E. Moore, the co-founder of Intel, made this observation that became known as Moore's Law.1
- Another tenet of Moore's Law says that the growth of microprocessors is exponential.
**Example**
_Pentium processor in 1993 had 3.1 million transistors, in 1995 the subsequent version of Pentium processor had 5.5 million transistors, and by 2003 the number had greatly expanded to 55 million this showcases the steady growth predicted by Moore’s law._
